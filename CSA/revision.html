Rapid Revision & Exam Success Guide

Condensed revision sheets for every time budget, plus a practical strategy for your final week, final day, and exam morning.

1-Day Revision Sheet

Use this if you have a full day before your exam. Spend roughly 45–60 minutes per block, with a 10-minute break between blocks.

Block 1 — Platform & Architecture: SaaS vs PaaS, multi-instance architecture, instance types (PDI, Dev, Test, Prod).
Block 2 — UI Fundamentals: Application Navigator, Modules vs Applications, Favorites vs History, Lists vs Forms, Related Lists.
Block 3 — Users, Groups, Roles: Role inheritance, direct vs group-based role assignment, scenario questions.
Block 4 — Tables: Task table hierarchy (Incident/Problem/Change), parent vs child, dictionary basics.
Block 5 — Field Types: String, Integer, Choice, Reference, Boolean, Date/Time, Journal — definitions and examples.
Block 6 — Service Catalog & Knowledge: Catalog hierarchy, REQ/RITM/SCTASK, Order Guides vs Record Producers, KB article lifecycle.
Block 7 — Flow Designer & Automation: Triggers, Actions, Subflows; Notifications and Events.
Block 8 — Update Sets & ACLs: Capture → Preview → Commit → Back Out; CRUD and ACL AND-logic evaluation.
Block 9 — Reporting & Imports: Chart types, Reports vs Dashboards vs PA, Import Set → Transform Map → Target Table.
Block 10 — Flashcards + 1 Mock Test: Run through all 150 flashcards once, then take one full Mock Test under timed conditions.

2-Hour Revision Sheet

Focused, high-yield review for the morning of or the night before your exam.

(20 min) Skim the Top 60 Exam-Critical Concepts in Module 16 of the Theory Guide.
(20 min) Review Update Sets: Capture → Preview → Commit → Back Out, and the "config not data" rule.
(20 min) Review ACL AND-logic: all applicable ACLs must pass; table-level vs field-level distinctions.
(20 min) Review Task table hierarchy and the benefit of table extension.
(20 min) Review Service Catalog terms: Catalog/Category/Item, REQ/RITM/SCTASK, Order Guide vs Record Producer.
(20 min) Run through Flashcards filtered to "Show only unknown" to target your weak spots.

30-Minute Revision Sheet

For right before you walk in. Just the highest-leverage reminders.

ACLs use AND logic — all applicable ACLs must pass, not just one.
Update Sets move configuration, never transactional data. Preview ≠ Commit.
Incident, Problem, Change all extend Task — shared fields/logic live there.
History = automatic recent records. Favorites = manual bookmarks.
Roles can come from direct assignment OR group membership — removing one source doesn't remove the other.
Ordering a Catalog Item creates REQ → RITM → SCTASK. A Record Producer skips this and writes directly to a target table.
Knowledge Articles: Draft → Review → Published → Retired. Only Published is visible to end users.
Reference fields display a friendly value but store a sys_id internally.
Journal fields are append-only — entries can't be edited or deleted.
Import Sets are always a staging table, never the final destination.

What to Study First

Start with the foundational, highest-frequency topics before niche ones: Platform Overview, UI basics, Users/Groups/Roles, and the Task table hierarchy. These underpin nearly every scenario question elsewhere in the exam.

Last Week Strategy

1
Complete all 16 theory modules at least once, marking each "complete" as you go to track progress.
2
Run through all 150 flashcards twice — once normally, once filtered to "Show only unknown."
3
Take Mock Test 1 under realistic timed conditions and review every explanation, even for questions you got right.
4
Identify your 2–3 weakest modules from mock test mistakes and re-read those theory sections.
5
Take Mock Tests 2 and 3 on different days, leaving at least a day between attempts to let review sink in.

Last Day Strategy

  • Don't cram new material — focus only on review and reinforcement.
  • Use the 2-Hour Revision Sheet above for a focused final pass.
  • Re-take your weakest-scoring Mock Test one more time to build confidence.
  • Get full sleep — recall performance drops significantly when sleep-deprived.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake: Memorizing definitions without understanding the underlying "why" — CSA scenario questions test application, not just recall.
Mistake: Skipping the ACL AND-logic and Update Set Preview/Commit distinctions — these are among the most frequently tested traps.
Mistake: Ignoring UI navigation terminology (Module vs Application, History vs Favorites) because it "seems too basic" — it's tested directly.
Mistake: Not reviewing wrong answers' explanations after mock tests — the explanation is often where the real learning happens.

Time Management During the Real Exam

  • Skim through and answer every question you're confident about first.
  • Flag uncertain questions and return to them after a first full pass.
  • Don't dwell more than 60–90 seconds on any single question on your first pass.
  • For scenario questions, re-read carefully for qualifier words like "only," "always," or "never" — these often determine the correct answer.
